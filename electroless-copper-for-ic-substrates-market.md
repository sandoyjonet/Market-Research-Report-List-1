<p><p>Electroless copper plating for IC substrates involves two primary market types: horizontal and vertical electroless copper. Horizontal electroless copper is characterized by its layout, where substrates are processed in a horizontal plane, enabling uniform deposition and efficient handling. This method is often favored for large-scale production. In contrast, vertical electroless copper involves a vertical arrangement of substrates, which optimizes space and allows for better fluid flow, enhancing deposition control for intricate designs. Both methods cater to diverse substrate requirements in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
