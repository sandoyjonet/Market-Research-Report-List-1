<p><p>The Enhancement Mode MOSFET market is divided into N-Type and P-Type segments, each characterized by their distinct functionalities. N-Type Enhancement Mode MOSFETs use electrons as charge carriers, offering high-speed operation and low on-resistance, predominantly suited for high-demand applications in power electronics and digital circuits. In contrast, P-Type Enhancement Mode MOSFETs utilize holes as carriers, providing complementary functionality for designing high-voltage and low-noise systems. Both types drive advancements in power management, automotive, and consumer electronics applications.</p></p>
